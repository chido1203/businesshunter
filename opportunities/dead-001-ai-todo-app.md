# DEAD OPPORTUNITY #001
## "An AI-powered to-do app"

**Why builders keep trying it:** Everyone uses task managers. AI can prioritize tasks automatically. Sounds obvious.

**Why it's dead:**

- **Notion, Todoist and Linear already have AI features** — built by teams of 50+ engineers with millions of users. You can't out-feature them.
- **No clear monetization** — people expect task apps to be free or $3/month. Your CAC will be higher than your LTV from day one.
- **Zero switching cost in your favor** — people already have years of tasks, workflows and team habits in existing tools. They will not move.
- **The "better X" trap** — "better Notion but with AI" is not a business. It's a feature request to Notion.

**The signal that fools people:** Reddit threads saying "I wish my todo app was smarter." That's not demand — that's a feature wish. Nobody is paying for it.

**The real opportunity nearby:** Niche task management for ONE specific profession (surgeons, architects, lawyers) with workflow built around their specific process. Narrow is survivable. General is a graveyard.

**Verdict:** Do not build. The market is saturated at every price point and the distribution moat is unbeatable without $10M+ in funding.
